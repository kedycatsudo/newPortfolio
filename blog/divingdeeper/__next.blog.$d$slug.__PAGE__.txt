## Frontend Design Wrapped üéâ ‚Äî MyFinance Dev Update

I can hardly believe it‚Äôs only been 12 days since I started building MyFinance. (To be honest, it felt even longer for how much I processed in those days. The result? ![MyFinance Logo](/newPortfolio/images/gallery/myfinancelogo.png)

---

### Starting with System Design

As I moved into frontend design, some of those choices needed realignment‚Äîwhich just proves how closely backend and frontend play together. ![Puzzle Logo](/newPortfolio/images/gallery/puzzle.jpg)

---

### The Evolution (What I Had vs. What I Changed)

Page by page, my design instincts grew. I kept asking myself: what do I really need to see (not just what would be ‚Äúcool‚Äù)? I focused on showing crisp separations between information blocks‚Äîthis is a data-driven app, after all, with not much imagery or fluff. Each block needed clear segmenting, so data is readable (and, dare I say, a little inviting?). By the final screen, I noticed I‚Äôd developed a consistent style for lists, inputs, and modals‚Äîso I looped back and updated the earlier pages for a unified MVP look.

---

### The Finished Design (So Far)

Here‚Äôs the current lineup of 5 main pages (and what changed):

- **Login/Register**  
  Totally refreshed with new input styles and containers. These now fit the background narrative and feel much more futuristic‚Äîso good, they‚Äôre almost ‚Äúfun‚Äù to fill out!
  
  ![Login/Register](/newPortfolio/images/gallery/loginregister.png)

- **Dashboard**  
  Updated data blocks with my current design language. I plopped in new "side" blocks that display investments and miscellaneous spending for the current month‚Äîturns out, these were just what the page needed. Recent incomings and outgoings show up in up-to-date tables.
  
  ![Dashboard](/newPortfolio/images/gallery/dashboard.png)

- **Incomes/Outcomes**  
  These pages are basically the birthplace of the new style. At first, the content was there, but it looked bland. With a new component system and better background, both pages are way cleaner and actually nice to look at, no matter how much data you throw at them.  
  ![Income/Outcome](/newPortfolio/images/gallery/incomesoutcomes.png)

- **Investment**  
  Here‚Äôs where things got really interesting. There‚Äôs a LOT more relevant data to surface than I first thought‚Äîassets, profit/loss, types, and more. I intentionally kept the scope tight so I wouldn‚Äôt overbuild, but re-used small components for new types of info. I started out building with MongoDB, but the deeper I went, the clearer it became‚Äîthis project‚Äôs structure begs for a relational database. I‚Äôm now researching my options:  
    - **PostgreSQL:** Handles relationships, powerful queries, open source, huge community.  
    - **MySQL:** Mature, widespread, great performance, but not as flexible for JSON or modern types.  
    - **Supabase:** Postgres-based with instant APIs/auth, but still a bit new for mission-critical stuff.  

  I haven‚Äôt picked one yet, but diving into relational DBs makes this project even more valuable for me‚ÄîI‚Äôm learning Next.js and TypeScript, then adding serious SQL design to my stack!

  ![Investment](/newPortfolio/images/gallery/investment.png)

- **Profile**  
  The simplest page, showing only what I need from the user: username, email (for auth/comms), the reset date for their monthly statement, and base currency for future features.

  ![Profile](/newPortfolio/images/gallery/profile.png)

---

- **Modals for Editing Data:**  
  There‚Äôs one main modal for editing, but it‚Äôs super reusable‚Äîswapping content as needed from page to page, styled for clarity and easy option selection or confirmation.

- **A Bunch of Small, Reusable Components:**  
  Core to keeping things DRY and maintainable.  
  ![Components](/newPortfolio/images/gallery/component.png)

- **Custom Buttons and Input Fields:**  
  Unified style, with color feedback and clear affordances everywhere.

In all honesty, designing for the large screen is now DONE‚Äîthe structure is locked in, and every part fits just right. All that‚Äôs left is implementation, testing, and deployment. It feels wild to write that!
